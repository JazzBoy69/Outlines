OUR NEW DRAMA HELPS US TO LEARN ABOUT THE LAND OF JESUS (0:00-2:00)
The Gospel accounts give us a glimpse of what life was like in Israel when Jesus was on earth
At the same time, they stimulate our curiosity about a time and a land that were perhaps quite different from our own
This symposium will help us to understand the environment and culture of Israel in Jesus’ day as portrayed in the video The Good News According to Jesus
Much of today’s popular entertainment teaches a person to be passive rather than active. Movies, books, and television programs present another person’s creative ideas, not your own. While there is a place for passive entertainment, many have found active entertainment to be more rewarding.
We will consider seven aspects of life in the land of Jesus​—geography, animals, food, homelife, community, education, and worship—​and we will draw valuable lessons from each one
Many aspects of life in Israel were guided by God’s Law
When the Israelites obeyed Jehovah and worshipped him faithfully, they benefited
THE GEOGRAPHY OF THE LAND OF JESUS (2:00-4:00)
The Promised Land had rivers, lakes, seashores, mountains, forests, and deserts​—all in a relatively small area [Read Deuteronomy 8:7]
For Jehovah your God is bringing you into a good land, a land of streams of water, springs and fountains flowing in the valley plain and in the mountainous region,

THE land that God gave to ancient Israel includes an extraordinary variety of geographic features. In the north are snowcapped mountains; areas in the south are tropical. Within its borders are productive lowlands, hill country for orchards and the grazing of flocks, as well as areas of desolate wilderness. Here in a relatively small area is a cross section of geographic features of the earth.

At its peak, the Promised Land with all its diversity was like the garden of Eden. Israel thus provided a small-scale example of what Paradise under the Kingdom of God will mean for mankind in all parts of the earth.

Although two thousand years have passed since Jesus’ time, the basic features of the land remain the same
Visitors to the land of Jesus can walk the same hills and valleys that Jesus walked
They can visit locations where he taught the people and performed miracles
All who are working on the video project The Good News According to Jesus are taking great care to portray the land accurately
As you watch the following video, note some aspects of the land where Jesus lived

LESSONS FROM THE STUDY OF THE GEOGRAPHY OF THE LAND OF JESUS (8:00-13:00)
Learning about the geography of the land of Jesus helps us to understand certain Bible expressions, illustrations, and accounts
When teaching, Jesus mentioned details about places that his listeners were familiar with
Because of the hilly terrain, people spoke of “going up” or “going down” to a place
People spoke of going up to Jerusalem. Here people speak of going up to Cuzco or down to the beach
For his parable of the neighborly Samaritan, he chose a place his listeners would be familiar with the road from Jerusalem to Jericho. Lu 10:31: a priest was going down on that road, away from Jerusalem, he had finished his assignment, there was no reason not to stop and help the traveler
Joseph, Mary, and their family “went up” from Nazareth to the Passover festival in Jerusalem​—an arduous journey that took several days (Lu 2:41, 42)
41 Now his parents were accustomed to go from year to year to Jerusalem for the festival of the Passover. 42 And when he was 12 years old, they went up according to the custom of the festival.
They were willing to exert themselves to share in true worship
We benefit when we imitate their example
Some aspects of our service to Jehovah might be easy for us. Like walking on level ground
How do we handle other aspects of our service that are more difficult for us? Ones that are like climbing hills and descending into deep valleys?
If year after year it is your custom to “go up,” to exert yourself and please Jehovah even when it involves doing something that is difficult for you, Jehovah will surely reward you for your sacrifices!
