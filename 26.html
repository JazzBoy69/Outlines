NO. 26 Why Are You Anxious?
PLEASE NOTE: This talk includes four video excerpts on the topic "STOP BEING ANXIOUS".
“STOP BEING ANXIOUS” (00:00-02:00)
Why? Stop. Why are you anxious. Stop being anxious.
Are you anxious about material things? Let’s open our Bibles to the verses noted in the program for this talk, Matthew 6:19-33, and we will see how to stop being anxious about material things
In verse 28 Jesus asked: “Why?” “Why are you anxious” about material things?
Some people are anxious to meet basic needs.
Verse 25: Anxiety about food and clothing was the motivation that Jesus addressed not greed
Others are anxious to get rich
Verse 24:Jesus talked about slaving for Riches
Still others are anxious to build a secure future for their family.
In the context in verse 34,Jesus mentioned being anxious about the future
Whatever the reason, Jesus counseled in verse 25: “Stop being anxious” about material things.
We need to 1) understand why not, why we don't need be anxious, then 2) stop.
WHY WE DO NOT NEED TO BE ANXIOUS ABOUT MATERIAL THINGS (02:00-10:00)

• In this context, anxiety is a symptom of a deeper problem. Anxiety comes from trusting in something or someone that we think is unreliable.

• If we trust in Riches, we will be anxious because they are unreliable.

Trusting in Riches is like riding in the car with someone who we know is a terrible driver. They just put a friend of yours in the hospital, and now you’re in their car.

• If we think Jehovah is unreliable, we will be anxious.

Thinking Jehovah is unreliable is like being in an airplane and not trusting the pilot. We will be anxious.

Jehovah will bless our efforts to obtain what we need if we put his interests first
Please watch and listen to what Jesus said as recorded at [Mt 6:31-33]

31 So never be anxious and say, ‘What are we to eat?’ or, ‘What are we to drink?’ or, ‘What are we to wear?’ 32 For all these are the things the nations are eagerly pursuing. We may go through some financial turbulence, but we can have peace, trusting that Jehovah will ensure that we have what we need.
Excessive concern for material things brings unnecessary suffering on us.
Merely aspiring to have more money can harm both mental and physical health.
The Bible long ago warned that the love of money is harmful and that it produces “many pains.”

HOW TO “STOP BEING ANXIOUS” ABOUT MATERIAL THINGS (10:00-22:00)
To stop being anxious, we must make friendship with Jehovah our priority.
What does this involve?
Please watch and listen to what Jesus said, as recorded at [Mt 6:19-21]
[Video crew begins excerpt from The Good News According to Jesus (0:45)]
Let’s examine Jesus’ words:
“Stop storing up for yourselves treasures on the earth”: To seek spiritual treasures, we must first stop storing up earthly treasures
“Rather, store up for yourselves treasures in heaven”: These spiritual treasures include all the good things that we do to please Jehovah and that are stored in his memory
Merely having a form of religion will not make a person spiritually rich any more than just having an empty bank account will make him financially wealthy; a person has to deposit (“store up”) money in the bank account
Cultivating a close relationship with God and developing qualities that he loves takes time, devotion, and sacrifice
“If, then, your eye is focused, your whole body will be bright”: A focused eye is fixed on one thing, not two or more
When we are focused on serving Jehovah we can have peace and less anxiety (“focused” study note on Mt 6:22, NWTSTY).
Trying to do too much causes anxiety
Doing something we know is wrong causes anxiety
We have more time and energy to help others spiritually, which brings us joy (Php 2:4)
Jesus made clear that we cannot successfully slave for both God and wealth
Please watch and listen to what Jesus said, as recorded at Matthew 6:24
[Video crew begins excerpt from The Good News According to Jesus (0:20)]
Note that Jesus did not merely advise against slaving for both God and Riches; he stated that it cannot be done
But we might think that we have to do both. We have our spiritual needs and our material concerns
Why did Jesus use the term “slave”?
He was illustrating what it means to love Jehovah our God with our whole soul
Jehovah is not harsh or demanding, but he does deserve exclusive devotion
We choose to become a slave of God when we dedicate our lives to him
If we think about it a slave does not have free time, he is slave a 24/7/365. He is not a slave on Tuesday night, Saturday morning, Sunday morning and then he is free the rest of the week.

Think about this example of a slave and his owner:

• Owner tells the slave: “I will take care of you and your family, but for now tend this garden and use the food for your family, but when I call you to do something else you have to put that first.”

• But the slave doesn't trust him, or doesn’t like the size of his garden. There is another slave with a bigger garden.

• The slave gets a side job so he can have as much as the other slave, and to put some money in the bank in case the owner doesn’t take care of him in the future.

• While he is working at his side job the owner looks for him, and the owner is not happy.

• He tells the slave to go do something, so he leaves his side job. The boss at his side job isn't happy

• When you are a slave you can’t have a side job. You can’t slave for two masters

You have to work, so why did Jesus say “Stop storing up treasures on the earth.” and “You can’t slave for Riches.”
If you are a slave of God, you know that he tells us to work hard to provide for ourselves and to have some left over for those in need.
Our job is not something separate from our service to God. It is part of our obedience to him.
It is like the garden that an owner gives to a slave so that he can grow food for his family.
So we could be working at a job as a slave for Riches, or we could be doing the same job and be a slave of Jehovah. How can we tell the difference?
Where your treasure is, there your heart will be also. Our heart. Our motives. Love of God or love or money? Do we trust in Jehovah or Riches? Two people can do the same thing for different reasons.
One person could work at a job to store up treasures on the earth, while another could work at the same job to store up treasures in heaven.
We have to sincerely trust Jehovah in our heart and follow his instructions if we are going to fulfill the promise we made when we dedicated our lives to him and said that we would in effect be his slaves.
When Jehovah comes looking for us, we want to be in the garden he gave us, not at a side job, because you cannot slave for God and for Riches.
Jehovah deserves our obedience all day every day. In that sense we are like his slaves.
But in this part of the Sermon on the Mount Jesus talked more about how Jehovah will take care of us like a Father
• Your heavenly Father knows you need all of these things. • What father whose son asks him for bread, food will give him a stone? • Trust that Jehovah will take care of you. He will care for you because he cares about you
Rather than worrying about exactly how Jehovah will keep his promise to care for us, we need to focus on the promise we made at our dedication to put him first (Mt 6:26-30)
WHAT ARE THE REWARDS OF PUTTING JEHOVAH FIRST? (22:00-24:00)
When we put Jehovah first, that is we decide to slave for God, trusting that he will provide for us if we obey him, we can let go of anxiety over material things.
With our focus on helping others spiritually, we enjoy the greater happiness of giving (Ac 20:35)
There is more happiness in giving than there is in receiving.
Our eternal happiness depends on keeping our promise to put Jehovah first
We can count on him to fulfill his promise to provide for us and to grant us everlasting life in the new world
hundredfold now and in the coming system everlasting life
Why are you anxious? Stop trusting in Riches to give you what only Jehovah can give.
Why are you happy? Because I trust in Jehovah.
