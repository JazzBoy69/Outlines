NO. 26 Why Are You Anxious?
PLEASE NOTE: This talk includes four video excerpts on the topic "STOP BEING ANXIOUS".
“STOP BEING ANXIOUS” (00:00-02:00)
Are you anxious about material things?
Jesus asked: “Why?” “Why are you anxious” about material things? (Mt 6:28)
Some people are anxious to meet basic needs.
Some are anxious to become rich.
Some are anxious to build a secure future for their family.
Whatever the reason, Jesus counseled: “Stop being anxious” about material things. He acknowledged that this type of anxiety is common, but that we need to stop. (Mt 6:25)
Is this guidance realistic today? If so, why?
WHY WE DO NOT NEED TO BE ANXIOUS ABOUT MATERIAL THINGS (02:00-10:00)
Jehovah will bless our efforts to obtain what we need if we put his interests first
Please watch and listen to what Jesus said as recorded at [Mt 6:31-33]
[Video crew begins excerpt from The Good News According to Jesus (0:40)]
31 So never be anxious and say, ‘What are we to eat?’ or, ‘What are we to drink?’ or, ‘What are we to wear?’ 32 For all these are the things the nations are eagerly pursuing. Your heavenly Father knows that you need all these things. 33“Keep on, then, seeking first the Kingdom and his righteousness, and all these other things will be added to you.
With these words, Jesus explained how to experience Jehovah’s care.
We may go through financial difficulty, but we can have peace, knowing that Jehovah will ensure that we have what we need.
Excessive concern for material things brings unnecessary suffering on us.
Merely aspiring to have more money can harm both mental and physical health (w21.08 18 ¶13).
The following video shows that Jehovah keeps his promise to care for those who seek him first

HOW TO “STOP BEING ANXIOUS” ABOUT MATERIAL THINGS (10:00-22:00)
To stop being anxious, we must make friendship with Jehovah our priority.
What does this involve?
Please watch and listen to what Jesus said, as recorded at [Mt 6:19-21]
[Video crew begins excerpt from The Good News According to Jesus (0:45)]
Let’s examine Jesus’ words:
“Stop storing up for yourselves treasures on the earth”: To seek spiritual treasures, we must first stop storing up earthly treasures
“Rather, store up for yourselves treasures in heaven”: These spiritual treasures include all the good things that we do to please Jehovah and that are stored in his memory
To seek spiritual treasures, we must first stop storing up earthly treasures.
Rather, store up treasures in heaven
These treasures include good deeds that please Jehovah and are remembered by him.
Having a form of religion is not enough, just as having an empty bank account does not make someone financially wealthy.
A person has to deposit (store up) spiritual money in heaven.
Cultivating a close relationship with Jehovah takes time, devotion, and sacrifice.
If, then, your eye is single, your whole body will be full of light
A focused eye is fixed on one thing, not on two or more.
When we are focused on serving Jehovah we can have peace and less anxiety (focused study note on Mt 6:22, NWTSTY).
We also have more time and energy to help others spiritually, which brings joy (Php 2:4).
Jesus made clear that we cannot successfully serve both God and wealth. Please listen to this excerpt from The Good News According to Jesus: [Mt 6:24] [0:20].
If a person tries to pursue both, one becomes primary and the other becomes a distraction.
[g 6/07 8 ¶2-3] That person will continue to be anxious.
We need to make a firm decision to put Jehovah first and give him exclusive devotion.
If material concerns have been competing with Jehovah for first place, we benefit from making needed adjustments.
Rather than worrying about how Jehovah will carry out his promise in each detail, focus on the promise made at dedication: to keep him first (Mt 6:26-30).
WHAT ARE THE REWARDS OF PUTTING JEHOVAH FIRST? (22:00-24:00)
When we put Jehovah first, we can let go of anxiety over material things.
By helping others spiritually, we experience the joy of giving (Ac 20:35).
By keeping our promise to put Jehovah first, we can count on him to fulfill his promise to provide for us.
We can also count on the reward of everlasting life in the new world.
